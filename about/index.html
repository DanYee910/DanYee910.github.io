About Me

Jan 13th, 2015 12:00 am

I was born in, raised in, and currently live in San Francisco.

I got interested in coding because of games. Rather, the potential to use coding to understand and make games. Arrays look like stashes with items inside of them, functions and methods look like attack() and openDoor(). The most fascinating part of coding though, is how it permeates everything in our world now. Coding has it’s fingers everywhere, it is inescapable and somebody at some point in time coded it.

I always thought of coding as rocket science, your brain needs to be wired in a very very strange way to talk it or understand it. After going through Dev Bootcamp, I will retract that a bit. Coding IS pretty complex and takes a certain mindset to wrap your head around. However, I think many people made the mistake I did and never gave it a chance. You can learn it, and you can make wonderful things with it. It is a true skill, that I think should be emphasized in schools as much as Algebra and English.

Besides coding, you can talk to me any time about:

Games:

I love games. On my PC that is currently Marvel Heroes and DotA2. Action RPGs are my favorite genre, MH is tops right now but before that came Diablo 2, Zy-El and Eastern Sun mods of D2, Titan Quest, Dungeon Defenders, Diablo 3, Borderlands 1 and 2, and Torchlight 1 and 2.

Off the keyboard I sit down all the time to board and card games. Dozens and dozens of board games, favorite probably being Galaxy Truckers. Magic: the Gathering I play regularly as well with friends.

Coins:

Slabbed and graded Chinese silver panda coins. Coins are awesome to me. On one hand it’s a hunk of metal, on the other it represents economy and finance, art, craftsmanship, history, etc… China changes the design of the silver panda every year, they look fantastic!

Archery:

I don’t exercise much, I guess if I’m using the body but not the brain I get bored? Olympic recurve archery is my one constant sport though, best sport ever. It has everything I want, incredibly sexy looking equipment, complex physics and biomechanics, great community, and everyone knows it’s awesome. I am aiming to get a level 2 instructor certification in the future, and hopefully be knowledgable and advanced enough later on to give something back to the sport.

History:

Military history specifically. I have a really really engrained tendency to sidetrack myself for HOURS on wikipedia, clicking links into articles about military history. Not limited to any time period or location either. I’ve wasted time reading up on Ancient Greece, Rome, China, all the way to colonial India and Africa, to wars of the 20th century. Whatever man, I think I am fascinated by the way such tumultuous events in the past have shaped history, changed borders, overturned languages, customs, and religions…
